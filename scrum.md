# Chapter 1 — What is Scrum?

---

# 🎤 Interview Question

**What is Scrum?**

---

## ⭐ Interview Answer (30–45 Seconds)

> **Scrum is a lightweight Agile framework used to manage and develop complex projects through short, time-boxed iterations called Sprints. It promotes collaboration, transparency, continuous feedback, and incremental delivery of working software. Scrum helps teams adapt to changing requirements while delivering value to customers frequently.**

---

## Short Answer (10–15 Seconds)

> **Scrum is an Agile framework that helps teams develop software in short iterations called Sprints while promoting collaboration, transparency, and continuous improvement.**

---

# What is a Framework?

Interviewers sometimes ask:

> **"Is Scrum a methodology?"**

### Correct Answer

> **No. Scrum is a framework, not a methodology. Agile is the broader philosophy, while Scrum provides a structured way to implement Agile practices.**

### Easy Way to Understand

Think of it like this:

- **Agile** = Philosophy (the mindset)
- **Scrum** = Framework (the way to apply that mindset)

---

# Why Was Scrum Introduced?

Traditional software development faced problems such as:

- Long delivery cycles
- Poor communication
- Late customer feedback
- Difficulty handling changes

Scrum was introduced to solve these challenges by delivering software in **small, manageable increments** and encouraging frequent inspection and adaptation.

---

# Key Characteristics of Scrum

- Time-boxed Sprints
- Small cross-functional teams
- Frequent delivery
- Customer feedback
- Transparency
- Continuous improvement
- Self-organizing teams

---

# Core Idea of Scrum

Instead of delivering software after 12 months:

```text
12 Months
      ↓
Complete Product
```

Scrum delivers value every Sprint:

```text
Sprint 1 → Login

Sprint 2 → Search

Sprint 3 → Cart

Sprint 4 → Payment

Sprint 5 → Order Tracking
```

Each Sprint produces a **potentially releasable product increment**.

---

# Scrum Framework Overview

```text
Product Backlog
        │
        ▼
Sprint Planning
        │
        ▼
Sprint (1–4 Weeks)
│
├── Development
├── Daily Scrum
├── Testing
│
▼
Sprint Review
        │
        ▼
Sprint Retrospective
        │
        ▼
Next Sprint
```

This cycle repeats until the product is complete.

---

# Scrum Goals

Scrum helps teams:

- Deliver working software quickly.
- Adapt to changing requirements.
- Improve product quality.
- Increase customer satisfaction.
- Reduce project risk.
- Encourage teamwork.

The product improves continuously based on feedback.

# Scrum Values

Interviewers occasionally ask:

> **"What are the Scrum Values?"**

There are **five Scrum Values**:

1. Commitment
2. Courage
3. Focus
4. Openness
5. Respect

You don't need to memorize detailed definitions for most fresher interviews, but you should know their names.

# Common Interview Questions

## Q1. Is Scrum the same as Agile?

### Answer

> **No. Agile is the philosophy, while Scrum is one of the most widely used frameworks for implementing Agile practices.**

---

## Q2. Why do companies use Scrum?

### Answer

> **Companies use Scrum because it enables frequent software delivery, adapts to changing requirements, improves collaboration, reduces project risk, and encourages continuous customer feedback.**

---

## Q3. What is the main objective of Scrum?

### Answer

> **The main objective of Scrum is to deliver high-quality working software in small increments while continuously adapting to customer needs.**

---

## Q4. How long is a Scrum Sprint?

### Answer

> **A Sprint typically lasts between one and four weeks. Most teams use two-week Sprints, but the duration depends on the project's needs.**

---

## Q5. What is delivered at the end of every Sprint?

### Answer

> **A potentially releasable product increment that has been completed, tested, and meets the team's Definition of Done.**

# Scrum vs Agile

| Agile | Scrum |
|--------|--------|
| Philosophy / Mindset | Framework |
| Based on values and principles | Implements Agile using defined roles, events, and artifacts |
| Many frameworks exist | One specific Agile framework |
| Flexible approach | Structured process with defined ceremonies |

---

# Common Mistakes

❌ **"Scrum and Agile are the same."**

Correct:

> Scrum is an Agile framework.

---

❌ **"Scrum is a programming language."**

Correct:

> Scrum is a project management framework.

---

❌ **"Scrum only works for software."**

Correct:

> Scrum originated in software development but is also used in marketing, product development, finance, education, and many other industries.
