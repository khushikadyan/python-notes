# Chapter 1 — What is Agile?

## Interview Question

**What is Agile Methodology?**

### ⭐ Interview Answer (30–45 seconds)

Agile is an iterative and incremental software development methodology that focuses on delivering small, working pieces of software frequently rather than waiting until the end of the project. It emphasizes collaboration, customer feedback, adaptability to changing requirements, and continuous improvement. Agile teams work in short development cycles called Sprints, allowing them to deliver value quickly and respond effectively to change.

### Short Answer (10–15 seconds)

Agile is a software development methodology that delivers software in small iterations while encouraging collaboration, customer feedback, and adaptability to changing requirements.

---

Try to naturally include these words:

- Iterative
- Incremental
- Customer Feedback
- Collaboration
- Continuous Improvement
- Flexibility
- Working Software
- Sprint
- Adaptability

# Understanding Agile

Imagine a company wants to build an online shopping application.

Instead of spending one year building the entire application before showing it to the customer, Agile breaks the project into small, manageable pieces.

For example:

### Sprint 1:

- User registration
- Login

### Sprint 2:

- Product listing
- Search

### Sprint 3:

- Shopping cart

### Sprint 4:

- Payment

### Sprint 5:

- Order tracking

After every Sprint, the customer reviews the working software and provides feedback. The team incorporates this feedback into future Sprints.

This allows changes to be made early instead of waiting until the end of the project.

---

# What does "Iterative and Incremental" Mean?

This is a favorite follow-up question.

## Iterative

**Meaning:** Improve the product through repeated cycles.

### Example:

Version 1: Basic login.

Version 2: Forgot password.

Version 3: Two-factor authentication.

The same feature improves over multiple iterations.

---

## Incremental

**Meaning:** Build the product in small, usable parts.

### Example:

- Login
- Search
- Cart
- Payment

Each increment adds new functionality while keeping the product usable.

---

## Interview Tip

**Iterative = Improve existing features.**

**Incremental = Add new features.**

# Why Was Agile Introduced?

## Interview Question

**Why was Agile developed?**

### Interview Answer

Agile was introduced to overcome the limitations of traditional software development models, such as the Waterfall model. In Waterfall, changing requirements late in the project was difficult and expensive. Agile allows teams to adapt to changing customer needs, deliver software faster, receive continuous feedback, and reduce project risk by developing software in small iterations.

---

# Problems with Traditional Development

Older models often suffered from:

- Long development cycles
- Late customer feedback
- High cost of changes
- High project failure risk
- Limited flexibility
- Late testing
- Delayed product delivery

Agile addresses these challenges through shorter development cycles and continuous feedback.

# What is the Agile Manifesto?

## 🎤 Interview Question

**What is the Agile Manifesto?**

### ⭐ Interview Answer (30–45 seconds)

The Agile Manifesto is a document created in 2001 by a group of software professionals to improve software development practices. It defines four core values and twelve guiding principles that emphasize collaboration, flexibility, customer satisfaction, and continuous delivery of working software. The Agile Manifesto serves as the foundation for Agile methodologies such as Scrum, Kanban, and Extreme Programming (XP).

### Short Answer (10–15 seconds)

The Agile Manifesto is a set of four values and twelve principles that guide Agile software development by promoting flexibility, collaboration, and continuous delivery of value.

---

# Why Was the Agile Manifesto Created?

Before Agile, most organizations followed the Waterfall Model, where:

- Requirements were fixed at the beginning.
- Development took months or years.
- Customers saw the product only at the end.
- Changing requirements was difficult and expensive.

A group of experienced software developers believed there was a better way.

In 2001, they met in Utah, USA, and created the Agile Manifesto, introducing a more flexible and collaborative approach to software development.

---

# The Four Agile Values

The Agile Manifesto consists of four core values.

A key point to remember:

The items on the left are valued more, but the items on the right are still important.

Interviewers often ask:

> **"Can you explain the four Agile values?"**

You don't need to memorize them word for word, but you should understand their meaning.

---

# Value 1

## Individuals and Interactions over Processes and Tools

### What does it mean?

People build software—not processes or tools.

Good communication and teamwork are more important than relying only on processes or software tools.

Processes and tools are still useful, but they should support people, not replace them.

### Real-World Example

Imagine two development teams.

### Team A

- Excellent Jira workflow
- Excellent documentation
- Poor communication
- Developers rarely collaborate

**Project Result:**

- ❌ Delays
- ❌ Misunderstandings

### Team B

- Regular discussions
- Strong teamwork
- Quick problem-solving
- Clear communication

**Project Result:**

- ✅ Faster delivery
- ✅ Better software

### Interview Answer

This value emphasizes that effective communication and collaboration among team members are more important than depending solely on processes or tools. While tools are important, successful software development ultimately depends on people working well together.

---

# Value 2

## Working Software over Comprehensive Documentation

### Meaning

The most important measure of progress is working software.

Documentation is valuable, but producing extensive documentation without delivering functional software provides little value.

### Example

Suppose a company spends six months writing detailed documents but has no usable application.

Another company delivers a working login feature in two weeks, along with essential documentation.

The second approach provides value to the customer much sooner.

### Interview Answer

This value means that delivering working software is more valuable than producing excessive documentation. Agile teams still create documentation, but they prioritize software that customers can use.

### Interview Tip

Don't say:

> "Agile doesn't require documentation."

That's incorrect.

Instead say:

Agile values sufficient documentation, but prioritizes working software.

---

# Value 3

## Customer Collaboration over Contract Negotiation

### Meaning

Customers should actively participate throughout the project.

Instead of only discussing requirements at the beginning through contracts, Agile encourages continuous collaboration and feedback.

### Example

Imagine you're building an online banking application.

After Sprint 2, the customer requests biometric login.

Because the customer is involved continuously, the team can incorporate this feedback into future Sprints.

### Interview Answer

This value emphasizes continuous collaboration with the customer throughout the project rather than relying solely on initial contracts and fixed requirements. Regular feedback helps ensure the product meets customer expectations.

---

# Value 4

## Responding to Change over Following a Plan

### Meaning

Requirements often change during software development.

Agile teams welcome change if it improves the product.

Instead of rigidly following an outdated plan, they adapt based on customer needs and market changes.

### Example

You're developing a food delivery application.

Initially, the client requests:

- Login
- Cart
- Payment

Later, they decide they also need:

- Live order tracking

An Agile team can adjust future Sprints to include this feature without waiting until the end of the project.

### Interview Answer

This value encourages teams to adapt to changing requirements rather than strictly following a fixed plan. Responding to change helps deliver greater value to customers.

---

# Easy Way to Remember the Four Values

**People → Product → Partnership → Flexibility**

- **People** → Individuals and Interactions
- **Product** → Working Software
- **Partnership** → Customer Collaboration
- **Flexibility** → Responding to Change

# Real-World Example (Putting It All Together)

Imagine you're developing an e-commerce website.

Instead of:

- Following a rigid process

- Refusing requirement changes

An Agile team would:

- Collaborate closely.
- Deliver working features every Sprint.
- Meet regularly with the customer.
- Adapt when new requirements arise.

This reflects all four Agile values in practice.

# What are Agile Principles?

## 🎤 Interview Question

**What are the Agile Principles?**

### ⭐ Interview Answer (30–45 seconds)

The Agile Manifesto is supported by twelve guiding principles that describe how Agile teams should develop software. These principles focus on customer satisfaction, frequent delivery, collaboration, adaptability, technical excellence, simplicity, and continuous improvement. Together, they help Agile teams deliver high-quality software efficiently while responding to changing requirements.

---

# Principle 1

## Customer Satisfaction Through Early and Continuous Delivery

### Meaning

The highest priority is to satisfy the customer by delivering valuable software early and continuously.

Instead of waiting months for the final product, Agile delivers useful features regularly.

### Example

**Sprint 1:**

- Login

**Sprint 2:**

- Search

**Sprint 3:**

- Payment

The customer starts receiving value after the very first Sprint.

### Interview Answer

Agile focuses on satisfying customers by delivering working software frequently so they can start using features early and provide feedback throughout development.

---

# Principle 2

## Welcome Changing Requirements

### Meaning

Requirements often change.

Instead of resisting change, Agile teams welcome it—even late in development—if it improves customer value.

### Example

A customer requests Dark Mode after Sprint 3.

An Agile team evaluates and plans it for a future Sprint instead of rejecting the request.

### Interview Answer

Agile welcomes changing requirements because adapting to customer needs helps create a more valuable product.

---

# Principle 3

## Deliver Working Software Frequently

### Meaning

Software should be delivered in small, working increments.

Rather than releasing everything after a year, Agile teams deliver features every few weeks.

### Example

Every two weeks:

- Login
- Search
- Cart
- Payment

Each Sprint results in usable software.

### Interview Answer

Agile encourages frequent delivery of working software so customers receive value regularly and teams receive feedback early.

---

# Principle 4

## Business People and Developers Work Together Daily

### Meaning

Developers and business stakeholders should communicate regularly.

This reduces misunderstandings and helps ensure the product meets business needs.

### Example

Daily discussions between:

- Product Owner
- Developers
- Testers

This ensures everyone understands the requirements.

### Interview Answer

Regular collaboration between business stakeholders and developers ensures that the software aligns with customer expectations and business goals.

---

# Principle 5

## Build Projects Around Motivated Individuals

### Meaning

- Hire capable people.
- Trust them.
- Provide the support they need.
- Avoid unnecessary micromanagement.

### Interview Answer

Agile believes motivated and trusted team members are more productive and deliver better software.

---

# Principle 6

## Face-to-Face Communication is Most Effective

### Meaning

Direct communication reduces confusion.

Today, video meetings often serve the same purpose for distributed teams.

### Example

Instead of a long email thread, a 10-minute discussion quickly resolves a misunderstanding.

### Interview Answer

Agile encourages direct communication because it helps teams solve problems faster and reduces misunderstandings.

---

# Principle 7

## Working Software is the Primary Measure of Progress

### Meaning

Progress is measured by software that works—not by the number of documents written or meetings held.

### Bad Example

- 500 pages of documentation
- No working application

### Good Example

- Functional login feature
- Customer can actually use it

### Interview Answer

In Agile, the primary measure of progress is working software because it provides real value to customers.

---

# Principle 8

## Maintain a Sustainable Pace

### Meaning

Teams should work at a pace they can maintain over time.

Constant overtime and burnout reduce quality and productivity.

### Interview Answer

Agile promotes a sustainable work pace so teams can consistently deliver high-quality software without burnout.

---

# Principle 9

## Continuous Attention to Technical Excellence

### Meaning

- Write clean, maintainable code.
- Use good engineering practices.
- Refactor when needed.

### Example

Instead of writing quick fixes repeatedly, improve the codebase to make future development easier.

### Interview Answer

Agile encourages technical excellence because high-quality code is easier to maintain, extend, and debug.

---

# Principle 10

## Simplicity

### Meaning

Build only what is needed today.

Avoid unnecessary features or over-engineering.

### Example

If users only need email login today, don't spend time building ten different authentication methods unless there's a clear requirement.

### Interview Answer

Agile values simplicity by focusing on delivering only the necessary features, reducing unnecessary work and complexity.

---

# Principle 11

## Self-Organizing Teams

### Meaning

Teams decide how to complete their work.

Managers set goals, but the team chooses the best technical approach.

### Interview Answer

Agile teams are self-organizing, meaning they collaborate to decide how work should be completed without constant supervision.

---

# Principle 12

## Regular Reflection and Continuous Improvement

### Meaning

After each Sprint, the team reflects on:

- What went well?
- What didn't?
- How can we improve?

This is usually done during the Sprint Retrospective.

### Interview Answer

Agile teams regularly reflect on their work and continuously improve their processes, communication, and technical practices.

# What is the Waterfall Model?

## 🎤 Interview Question

**What is the Waterfall Model?**

### ⭐ Interview Answer (30–45 seconds)

The Waterfall Model is a traditional software development methodology in which development progresses through sequential phases, such as requirement gathering, design, development, testing, deployment, and maintenance. Each phase must be completed before moving to the next, making it difficult to accommodate changes once development has started.

### Short Answer

Waterfall is a linear software development model where each phase is completed before the next phase begins.

---

# Waterfall Lifecycle

```text
Requirements
      ↓
Design
      ↓
Development
      ↓
Testing
      ↓
Deployment
      ↓
Maintenance
```

Notice that there is no feedback loop between the phases.

---

# What is Agile?

You already studied this in Chapter 1.

Instead of completing the entire project at once, Agile divides it into small iterations (Sprints).

### Example:

**Sprint 1 →** Login

**Sprint 2 →** Search

**Sprint 3 →** Cart

**Sprint 4 →** Payment

**Sprint 5 →** Order Tracking

Each Sprint delivers working software.

---

# Agile vs Waterfall

This is one of the most frequently asked interview questions.

### ⭐ Interview Answer (1 Minute)

Agile and Waterfall are both software development methodologies, but they differ significantly in their approach. Waterfall follows a linear, sequential process where each phase is completed before moving to the next, making changes difficult later in the project. Agile follows an iterative and incremental approach, delivering software in small iterations while welcoming changing requirements and continuous customer feedback. Agile emphasizes flexibility, collaboration, and frequent delivery, whereas Waterfall emphasizes detailed planning and documentation before development begins.

---

# Difference Table

| Agile | Waterfall |
|--------|-----------|
| Iterative & Incremental | Linear & Sequential |
| Flexible | Rigid |
| Welcomes change | Changes are difficult |
| Frequent software delivery | Delivered at the end |
| Continuous customer feedback | Customer mainly involved at the beginning |
| Testing happens throughout | Testing usually happens after development |
| Better for changing requirements | Better for stable requirements |
| Lower risk due to early feedback | Higher risk if issues are found late |
| Team collaboration is continuous | Teams often work phase by phase |

Most IT companies prefer Agile because it allows faster delivery, continuous customer feedback, flexibility to adapt to changing requirements, and better collaboration among team members.

## Q5. Which model detects bugs earlier?

Agile generally detects bugs earlier because testing occurs throughout development, whereas in Waterfall testing is usually performed after the development phase.

- **T** → Team collaboration

### Waterfall:

Remember **P.L.A.N**

- **P** → Predictable phases
- **L** → Linear process
- **A** → All requirements defined early
- **N** → No easy changes

---

# 🎤 Interview Question

**What are the advantages of Agile Methodology?**

---

# ⭐ Interview Answer (45–60 seconds)

> **Agile offers several advantages. It enables faster delivery of working software through short iterations, encourages continuous customer feedback, and adapts easily to changing requirements. Regular testing throughout development helps identify issues early, reducing project risk. Agile also promotes strong collaboration among team members, improves product quality through continuous improvement, and increases customer satisfaction by delivering value frequently.**

---

# Top Advantages of Agile

---

# 1. Faster Delivery

### Meaning

Software is delivered in small increments rather than waiting until the end of the project.

---

### Example

Instead of waiting 12 months for the complete application,

The customer receives:

Working software is available after every Sprint.

One of Agile's biggest strengths.

Customers are involved throughout development.

### Example

Customer reviews the product every Sprint.

Feedback is incorporated into future Sprints.

### Interview Answer

Testing is performed continuously.

### Result

Testing occurs throughout the project instead of only after development.

# 7. Better Team Collaboration

# 8. Higher Customer Satisfaction

# 🎤 Interview Question

**What are the disadvantages of Agile?**

---

# ⭐ Interview Answer (45–60 seconds)

> **Although Agile has many benefits, it also has some limitations. Agile requires active customer involvement throughout the project, which may not always be possible. Frequent changes can lead to scope creep if they are not managed properly. Agile also requires experienced and collaborative teams, and estimating project cost and timelines can be more challenging because requirements evolve during development. Additionally, Agile may not be the best choice for projects with fixed requirements or strict regulatory documentation.**

---

# Top Disadvantages of Agile

---

# 1. Requires Continuous Customer Involvement

If the customer is unavailable,

the team may struggle to clarify requirements or prioritize work.

---

### Example

Customer doesn't attend Sprint Review.

Developers may build features based on assumptions.

---

# 2. Scope Creep

New features are frequently requested.

Without proper prioritization,

the project scope can keep expanding.

---

### Example

Customer requests:

- Chat
- Notifications
- AI Assistant

The project keeps growing.

---

### Interview Tip

Mention:

> **Scope creep can be controlled through backlog prioritization by the Product Owner.**

---

# 3. Difficult Cost and Time Estimation

Since requirements evolve,

predicting the exact project duration and budget can be difficult.

---

# 4. Requires Experienced Teams

Agile teams make many decisions independently.

Inexperienced teams may struggle with planning, communication, or prioritization.

---

# 5. Documentation May Be Lighter

Agile values working software over excessive documentation.

If documentation becomes too limited,

future maintenance can become difficult.

**Note:** Agile does **not** eliminate documentation—it aims for the right amount.

---

# 6. Not Suitable for Every Project

Some projects require:

- Fixed scope
- Heavy documentation
- Strict regulatory compliance

Waterfall or hybrid approaches may be more appropriate.

# What is the Agile Lifecycle?

## 🎤 Interview Question

**What is the Agile Lifecycle?**

### ⭐ Interview Answer (30–45 seconds)

> **The Agile Lifecycle is the step-by-step process followed by Agile teams to develop, deliver, and continuously improve software. Instead of completing the entire project at once, Agile develops the product in small iterations called Sprints. Each Sprint includes planning, development, testing, review, feedback, and improvement before the next Sprint begins.**

---

# Agile Lifecycle Phases

```text
Requirements
      ↓
Planning
      ↓
Sprint Planning
      ↓
Development
      ↓
Testing
      ↓
Sprint Review
      ↓
Sprint Retrospective
      ↓
Next Sprint (Repeat)
```

Unlike Waterfall, Agile repeats this cycle for every Sprint.

---

# Phase 1 — Requirements

## What happens?

- Customer shares requirements.
- Product Owner gathers and prioritizes them.
- Requirements are added to the Product Backlog.

### Example

Customer wants:

- Login
- Search
- Cart
- Payment

---

# Phase 2 — Planning

## What happens?

The team decides:

- What should be built first?
- Sprint goals
- Priorities
- Estimates

This prepares the team for Sprint Planning.

---

# Phase 3 — Sprint Planning

## What happens?

The team selects items from the Product Backlog and creates the Sprint Backlog.

Everyone agrees on:

- Sprint Goal
- Tasks
- Timeline

---

# Phase 4 — Development

## What happens?

Developers:

- Write code
- Review code
- Collaborate
- Update Jira
- Build features

### Example

**Sprint 1 → Login Feature**

---

# Phase 5 — Testing

## What happens?

QA engineers or developers:

- Test features
- Find bugs
- Fix defects
- Verify functionality

Testing happens throughout the Sprint, not just at the end.

---

# Phase 6 — Sprint Review

## What happens?

The team demonstrates the completed work to stakeholders.

Customer feedback is collected.

### Possible outcomes:

- Feature accepted
- Suggestions received
- New requirements added to the Product Backlog

---

# Phase 7 — Sprint Retrospective

## What happens?

The team discusses:

- What went well?
- What didn't go well?
- What should we improve in the next Sprint?

The goal is continuous improvement.

---

# Repeat the Cycle

After one Sprint ends:

- Next Sprint begins.
- New backlog items are selected.
- The process repeats until the product is complete.

---

# Real-World Example

Suppose you're building an online shopping app.

### Sprint 1

- Login
- Registration

**Review →** Customer requests OTP login.

### Sprint 2

- Search
- Product Listing
- OTP Login

**Review →** Customer requests Wishlist.

### Sprint 3

- Cart
- Wishlist

This cycle continues until the application is complete.

---

# Agile Lifecycle vs Waterfall Lifecycle

| Agile | Waterfall |
|--------|-----------|
| Repeats every Sprint | Happens once |
| Frequent feedback | Feedback mostly at the end |
| Continuous testing | Testing after development |
| Flexible | Less flexible |
| Incremental delivery | Final delivery at project completion |

---

# Common Interview Questions

## Q1. Why is the Agile Lifecycle iterative?

### Answer:

Because Agile develops software in repeated Sprints. After each Sprint, the team reviews feedback, improves the product, and begins the next iteration.

---

## Q2. What happens after a Sprint ends?

### Answer:

The team conducts a Sprint Review and Sprint Retrospective, updates the Product Backlog if needed, and starts planning the next Sprint.

---

## Q3. Is testing a separate phase in Agile?

### Answer:

No. Testing is integrated into every Sprint and occurs continuously alongside development.

---

## Q4. Why is customer feedback important in the Agile Lifecycle?

### Answer:

Customer feedback helps the team validate completed work, identify improvements early, and ensure the product continues to meet business needs.

---

**Correct:**

Agile includes planning, development, testing, review, feedback, and improvement.

---

**Correct:**

Testing is continuous throughout the Sprint.

---

❌ **"The Sprint Review and Sprint Retrospective are the same."**

**Correct:**

- **Sprint Review:** Focuses on the product and stakeholder feedback.
- **Sprint Retrospective:** Focuses on improving the team's way of working.
