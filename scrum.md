# Chapter 1 — What is Scrum?

---

# 🎤 Interview Question

**What is Scrum?**

---

## ⭐ Interview Answer (30–45 Seconds)

> **Scrum is a lightweight Agile framework used to manage and develop complex projects through short, time-boxed iterations called Sprints. It promotes collaboration, transparency, continuous feedback, and incremental delivery of working software. Scrum helps teams adapt to changing requirements while delivering value to customers frequently.**

---

## Short Answer (10–15 Seconds)

> **Scrum is an Agile framework that helps teams develop software in short iterations called Sprints while promoting collaboration, transparency, and continuous improvement.**

---

# What is a Framework?

Interviewers sometimes ask:

> **"Is Scrum a methodology?"**

### Correct Answer

> **No. Scrum is a framework, not a methodology. Agile is the broader philosophy, while Scrum provides a structured way to implement Agile practices.**

### Easy Way to Understand

Think of it like this:

- **Agile** = Philosophy (the mindset)
- **Scrum** = Framework (the way to apply that mindset)

---

# Why Was Scrum Introduced?

Traditional software development faced problems such as:

- Long delivery cycles
- Poor communication
- Late customer feedback
- Difficulty handling changes

Scrum was introduced to solve these challenges by delivering software in **small, manageable increments** and encouraging frequent inspection and adaptation.

---

# Key Characteristics of Scrum

- Time-boxed Sprints
- Small cross-functional teams
- Frequent delivery
- Customer feedback
- Transparency
- Continuous improvement
- Self-organizing teams

---

# Core Idea of Scrum

Instead of delivering software after 12 months:

```text
12 Months
      ↓
Complete Product
```

Scrum delivers value every Sprint:

```text
Sprint 1 → Login

Sprint 2 → Search

Sprint 3 → Cart

Sprint 4 → Payment

Sprint 5 → Order Tracking
```

Each Sprint produces a **potentially releasable product increment**.

---

This cycle repeats until the product is complete.

# What is the Scrum Framework?

## 🎤 Interview Question

**What is the Scrum Framework?**

---

## ⭐ Interview Answer (45–60 Seconds)

> **The Scrum Framework is a structured approach for implementing Agile principles. It organizes software development into short, time-boxed iterations called Sprints. Scrum consists of three roles, five events, and three artifacts that help teams collaborate, deliver working software frequently, gather customer feedback, and continuously improve their processes.**

---

## Short Answer

> **The Scrum Framework is a structured Agile framework that uses defined roles, events, and artifacts to deliver software in short iterations called Sprints.**

---

# Components of the Scrum Framework

Scrum has **three main components**:

### 1. Scrum Roles

Responsible for managing and developing the product.

- Product Owner
- Scrum Master
- Developers (Development Team)

---

### 2. Scrum Events

Activities that happen during every Sprint.

- Sprint
- Sprint Planning
- Daily Scrum
- Sprint Review
- Sprint Retrospective

---

### 3. Scrum Artifacts

Work items used to manage the project.

- Product Backlog
- Sprint Backlog
- Increment

# How Scrum Works (Step by Step)

---

## Step 1 — Product Backlog

The **Product Owner** creates and prioritizes a list of all project requirements.

### Example:

- Login
- Registration
- Search
- Cart
- Payment
- Wishlist

This list is called the **Product Backlog**.

---

## Step 2 — Sprint Planning

The Scrum Team decides:

- Which backlog items to complete.
- Sprint Goal.
- Tasks for the Sprint.

The selected items become the **Sprint Backlog**.

---

## Step 3 — Sprint Begins

A Sprint usually lasts **1–4 weeks**.

During the Sprint:

- Developers write code.
- Testers verify functionality.
- Team members collaborate.
- Daily Scrum meetings are held.

---

## Step 4 — Product Increment

At the end of the Sprint,

the team delivers a **working product increment**.

### Example:

Sprint 1 delivers:

- Login
- Registration

These features are complete, tested, and potentially releasable.

---

## Step 5 — Sprint Review

The team demonstrates the completed work to stakeholders.

The customer provides feedback.

### Example:

> **"Please add Google Login."**

This new request is added to the Product Backlog.

---

## Step 6 — Sprint Retrospective

The team reflects on:

- What went well?
- What didn't go well?
- How can we improve?

The focus is on improving the team's process for the next Sprint.

---

## Step 7 — Repeat

The cycle starts again with the next Sprint.

This iterative process continues until the product is complete.

- **Inspection:** The team regularly reviews the product and process.
- **Adaptation:** The team makes improvements based on feedback and inspection.

## Q3. How many Scrum events are there?

# What are Scrum Roles?

## 🎤 Interview Question

**What are the Scrum Roles?**

### ⭐ Interview Answer (30–45 Seconds)

> **Scrum has three roles: the Product Owner, the Scrum Master, and the Developers. The Product Owner is responsible for maximizing product value and managing the Product Backlog. The Scrum Master ensures that Scrum practices are followed and helps remove obstacles for the team. The Developers design, build, test, and deliver the product increment during each Sprint.**

# 1. Product Owner (PO)

## Who is the Product Owner?

The **Product Owner** represents the customer and business.

Their main responsibility is to **maximize the value of the product**.

---

## Responsibilities

- Owns the **Product Backlog**
- Prioritizes backlog items
- Defines business requirements
- Communicates with stakeholders
- Accepts or rejects completed work
- Clarifies requirements for the team

---

## Real-World Example

A banking application has these features:

- Login
- UPI Payment
- Loan Section
- Credit Card Module

The Product Owner decides:

> **"UPI Payment is the highest priority, so we'll build it before the Loan Section."**

---

## Interview Answer

> **The Product Owner represents the customer, manages and prioritizes the Product Backlog, defines business requirements, and ensures the team delivers maximum value to the customer.**

---

# 2. Scrum Master

## Who is the Scrum Master?

The **Scrum Master** is a servant leader and facilitator.

They ensure that the Scrum process is followed correctly.

---

## Responsibilities

- Facilitates Scrum events
- Coaches the team on Scrum practices
- Removes impediments (blockers)
- Protects the team from unnecessary interruptions
- Promotes continuous improvement
- Helps the team follow Agile principles

---

## Real-World Example

A developer cannot continue because access to the test server is unavailable.

The Scrum Master coordinates with the infrastructure team to resolve the issue so development can continue.

---

## Interview Answer

> **The Scrum Master ensures that Scrum practices are followed, facilitates Scrum events, removes obstacles affecting the team's progress, and helps the team continuously improve.**

---

# 3. Developers

## Who are the Developers?

Developers are the professionals responsible for creating the product.

This includes:

- Software Developers
- Test Engineers
- UI/UX Engineers
- DevOps Engineers (depending on the team structure)

---

## Responsibilities

- Design the solution
- Write code
- Test features
- Fix bugs
- Collaborate with teammates
- Deliver a working product increment
- Maintain quality standards

---

## Interview Answer

> **Developers are responsible for designing, building, testing, and delivering a working product increment during each Sprint. They collaborate with the Scrum Team to achieve the Sprint Goal.**

# What are Scrum Events?

> **Scrum has five events that help teams plan, execute, inspect, and improve their work. These are Sprint, Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, and Sprint Retrospective.**

---

## 1. Sprint

- Time-boxed iteration (usually **1–4 weeks**)
- Goal: Deliver a **working product increment**

**Example:**

Sprint 1 → Login & Registration

---

## 2. Sprint Planning

### Purpose

- Decide **what** to build.
- Decide **how** to build it.
- Define the **Sprint Goal**.

**Participants:**

- Product Owner
- Scrum Master
- Developers

---

## 3. Daily Scrum (Daily Stand-up)

### Purpose

A **15-minute daily meeting** where developers synchronize their work.

Typical questions:

- What did I complete yesterday?
- What will I work on today?
- Do I have any blockers?

**Purpose:** Improve communication and identify issues early.

---

## 4. Sprint Review

### Purpose

- Demonstrate completed work to stakeholders.
- Collect customer feedback.
- Update the Product Backlog if needed.

**Focus:** **Product**

---

## 5. Sprint Retrospective

### Purpose

The team reflects on:

- What went well?
- What didn't?
- How can we improve?

**Focus:** **Team and process improvement**

---

## Difference Between Sprint Review & Sprint Retrospective

| Sprint Review | Sprint Retrospective |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Focuses on the product | Focuses on the team's process |
| Stakeholders participate | Internal Scrum Team meeting |
| Collect customer feedback | Identify improvements for the next Sprint |

# What are Scrum Artifacts?

## 🎤 Interview Question

**What are Scrum Artifacts?**

### ⭐ Interview Answer

> **Scrum has three artifacts: Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, and Increment. These artifacts provide transparency about the work to be done, the work currently in progress, and the completed work delivered during each Sprint.**

---

## 1. Product Backlog

### Definition

A **prioritized list of all product requirements**.

Managed by:

- **Product Owner**

Contains:

- Features
- Enhancements
- Bugs
- Technical improvements

**Example:**

- Login
- Search
- Cart
- Payment
- Notifications

---

## 2. Sprint Backlog

### Definition

A list of Product Backlog items selected for the current Sprint, along with the tasks needed to complete them.

Created during:

- **Sprint Planning**

Managed by:

- **Developers**

### Example

Sprint 1

- Login
- Registration

---

## 3. Increment

### Definition

The completed, tested, and potentially releasable work delivered at the end of a Sprint.

### Example

Sprint 1 delivers:

- Login
- Registration

This is the **Increment**.

---

## Product Backlog vs Sprint Backlog

| Product Backlog | Sprint Backlog |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Entire project work | Current Sprint work |
| Managed by Product Owner | Managed by Developers |
| Changes over the product's lifetime | Changes during the current Sprint if needed to meet the Sprint Goal |

---

## Scrum Flow

```text
Product Backlog
       │
       ▼
Sprint Planning
       │
       ▼
Sprint Backlog
       │
       ▼
Development & Testing
       │
       ▼
Increment
```

---

## Quick Revision Table

| Artifact | Owner | Purpose |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Product Backlog | Product Owner | All project requirements |
| Sprint Backlog | Developers | Work selected for the current Sprint |
| Increment | Scrum Team | Completed working software |
